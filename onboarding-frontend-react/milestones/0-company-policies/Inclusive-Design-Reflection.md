#### 1Ô∏è‚É£ Who are considered vulnerable populations, and what challenges might they face in digital spaces?

Vulnerable populations include individuals who experience barriers in accessing or using digital tools effectively. This can include:

- Neurodivergent individuals (ADHD, Autism, Dyslexia)
- People with disabilities (visual, hearing, mobility impairments)
- Elderly users who may struggle with new technologies
- Individuals with mental health challenges (anxiety, depression)

** Challenges they face in digital spaces: **

- Overwhelming interfaces with too much information
- Difficult navigation due to cluttered layouts
- Lack of accessibility options (e.g., screen reader support, color contrast)
- Sensory overload from flashing visuals or loud sounds

For example, a website with constant pop-ups, bright colors, and moving elements can be stressful for someone with Autism who prefers a calm, predictable interface.

#### 2Ô∏è‚É£ What ethical considerations are important when designing or working with neurodivergent individuals? (Hint: Avoid overwhelming UX, respect sensory needs, ensure clear communication.)

- Avoid overwhelming UX ‚Äì Keep interfaces simple, structured, and easy to navigate.
- Respect sensory needs ‚Äì Allow users to customize colors, animations, and sounds.
- Ensure clear communication ‚Äì Use concise language, provide step-by-step instructions.
- Offer flexibility ‚Äì Different people process information in different ways.

For example, instead of using vague error messages like "Something went wrong!", provide clear instructions like "Please check your internet connection and try again."

#### 3Ô∏è‚É£ How can you make interactions and content more accessible for people with ADHD or Autism? (e.g., simple language, predictable navigation, reducing cognitive load)

- Use simple and direct language ‚Äì Avoid unnecessary complexity.
- Ensure predictable navigation ‚Äì Keep menus, buttons, and layouts consistent.
- Reduce cognitive load ‚Äì Use clear headings, bullet points, and visual cues.
- Allow personalization ‚Äì Let users adjust font sizes, colors, and animations.

For example, a productivity app can include a "focus mode" that hides distracting elements and minimizes notifications for users with ADHD.

#### 4Ô∏è‚É£ How can we support neurodivergent team members in a professional setting? (Hint: Be clear in communication, respect different working styles, and allow flexibility.)

- Be clear in communication ‚Äì Provide written summaries after meetings to reinforce key points.
- Respect different working styles ‚Äì Some may prefer async communication over real-time discussions.
- Allow flexibility ‚Äì Give autonomy in work schedules and breaks to accommodate focus needs.
- Minimize unnecessary meetings ‚Äì Many neurodivergent individuals prefer focused deep work.

For example, if a team member struggles with verbal processing, sending key discussion points in advance can help them prepare and contribute effectively.
